# Audit Memo: Why Tenacious-Bench Is Needed

tau2-Bench retail is useful for measuring whether an agent can complete transactional workflows under tool constraints. It does not measure whether a B2B sales agent should have sent a message in the first place, whether the pitch matches a company's buying window, or whether a claim is grounded in public signal. The Week 10 `trace_log.jsonl` confirms this mismatch: those records are retail-domain simulations with rewards, costs, durations, and task IDs. They say almost nothing about Tenacious-specific sales quality. A retail success can coexist with a sales failure where a prospect receives a polished but wrong email.

The Tenacious challenge is narrower and more dangerous: the system enriches public signals into briefs, selects an ICP segment, writes outbound email, interprets replies, and routes follow-up. The failure is not mostly grammar or completion. It is semantic fit. The style guide requires direct, grounded, honest, professional, non-condescending outreach, but Week 10 showed that surface checks can pass while the substance is wrong.

The clearest evidence is the contrast between SnapTrade, WiseiTech, and the clean Segment 1 traces. In `artifacts/snaptrade/email_log.jsonl`, the email cites a real negative hiring signal: job postings decreased 60% in 60 days. The corresponding brief marks SnapTrade as `icp_segment=ambiguous`, with `hiring_velocity.delta_pct=-60.0` and low budget urgency. Yet the email says companies in that position face "bottlenecks integrating new APIs" and that Tenacious can "augment your team and accelerate API development." It received `tone_warnings: []`. The surface validator saw a short, polite email; the buyer would see an unsupported growth pitch to a company with contracting public hiring signal.

WiseiTech repeats the pattern more sharply. Its brief shows `delta_pct=-100.0`, `signal_strength=weak`, `icp_segment=ambiguous`, and `weak_hiring_velocity_signal=true`. The email still says companies in its position need to "augment existing Python, Big Data, and ML capabilities" and offers "research-backed engineers available on-demand." Again the tone warning list is empty. These two traces prove that the Week 10 system can confuse stack match with ICP fit. A company having Python or ML in its stack is not evidence that it is in market for staff augmentation.

The positive examples show the intended boundary. Arcana Analytics had `delta_pct=100.0`, a recent Series A, `icp_segment=Segment 1`, and a pitch about scaling ML engineering after funding. PulseSight had `delta_pct=133.33`, Series A funding, and a scaling pitch for ML infrastructure. StreamlineOps had `delta_pct=120.0`, Series B funding, urgent Kafka/Spark demand, and available bench match. These are credible growth-window pitches because velocity, funding, ICP segment, and stack all point in the same direction.

The probe library adds evidence that generic benchmarks miss the risky edges. Probes 8-10 target ICP misclassification: layoffs, fully staffed teams, and NestJS stack questions must override the original segment. Probes 11-13 target signal over-claiming: when a prospect challenges "doubling," stale funding, or role-type mismatch, the system must acknowledge the limit rather than defend the original claim. Probes 18-20 show tone drift around self-disclosed AI gaps; even a warm lead can be lost if the system becomes condescending. Probes 24-26 cover data provenance and weak-signal honesty. Probe 30 combines a positive hiring signal, a NestJS bench trap, and pricing in one reply, proving that real sales conversations do not isolate dimensions.

The core benchmark problems are therefore ICP-pitch alignment and signal directionality, with grounding fidelity as the base requirement. If a brief says "Ambiguous," the email should ask a qualifying question, not make a product claim. If hiring velocity is materially negative, the email must not use growth frames such as scaling, bottlenecks, acceleration, or augmentation unless the brief provides a restructuring-specific reason. If the email contains numeric, date, funding, stack, or capacity claims, those claims must be traceable to the brief, bench summary, pricing sheet, or prior thread.

Tenacious-Bench must measure five dimensions. D1 Grounding Fidelity checks whether claims are supported by supplied evidence. D2 ICP-Pitch Alignment checks whether the pitch frame matches Segment 1, 2, 3, 4, or an abstention path for Ambiguous. D3 Signal Directionality catches growth language on negative hiring velocity. D4 Tone Compliance turns style-guide warnings into hard failures. D5 Format Compliance enforces subject length, body length, approved outreach structure, and no cold booking links. Together these dimensions make the hidden Week 10 failure machine-verifiable: a stranger can score whether a short, clean email is actually safe to send.

## What This Enables for Phase 2 (Dataset Construction)

This audit turns the Week 10 traces and probes into authoring rules. Phase 2 can now build trace-derived, programmatic, synthetic, and hand-adversarial tasks around explicit dimensions, with deterministic labels for grounding, directionality, tone, and format, and a later LLM-judged layer only where semantic ICP-pitch alignment cannot be reduced safely to string checks.
